I'm a joint PhD candidate in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Princeton Neuroscience Institute at Princeton University working with Prof. Peter Ramadge and Prof. Uri Hasson. My primary research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning and computational neuroscience. The current focus is on developing machine learning models for handling inter-subject variability to utilize multi-subject human brain data with a hope to learn more about how the brain works. Besides, I have also worked on several theoretical/applied machine learning projects in various domains at/with Princeton, Amazon, Palantir, and Intel Labs.

Before coming to Princeton, I received my BS in Electrical Engineering (valedictorian) from National Taiwan University.

I have been awarded Google PhD Fellowship. Thanks to Google for supporting my research!
